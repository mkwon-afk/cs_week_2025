💡 Customer Journey Redesign Insights

Question: "If you could redesign part of our customer journey, what would you focus on first?"

Discover what our Red Hat Support team believes would make the biggest impact on customer experience

🎯 First Contact & Routing

"The customer should be able to contact the team they are looking for on the first try, rather than jumping through hoops to get where they need."
"I think if customer opts for 'General Inquiry' while submitting the case then he/she should be able to see all Red Hat products along with their version but if customer opts for 'Issue' related to the purchased product entitlements then he/she should be able to list those products only according to the entitlements."
"Ease of connecting to customer service executive or engineer lightening fast."

🚀 Onboarding & First Impressions

"I would focus first on the onboarding experience — the first interaction after a customer has committed. It's the most critical moment to validate their purchase, build confidence, and proactively guide them to success."
"AI Personal Onboarding Coach: Guides the next best action by role and behavior, answers in natural language, surfaces tailored tips and best practices."
"A clear, guided onboarding experience ensures customers understand how to contact support/ utilize great resources available through our portal/kcs articles."

🤝 Proactive Support & Communication

"Implementing proactive outreach, where we anticipate and notify customers about known issues or maintenance before they become problems."
"I'd redesign the early engagement touchpoints to be more proactive, reducing confusion and improving trust."
"The greeting, it could change after a certain time to not feel repetitive."

🔄 Seamless Handoffs & Collaboration

"I'd focus on making support handoffs seamless, ensuring knowledge and context flow smoothly between engineers so customers always feel continuity, collaboration, and care."
"I would align levels for support engineers. Level 1, 2 and 3 engineers should handle the case as per the criticality and complexity."
"collaboration between different teams"

⚡ Efficiency & Self-Service

"I'd focus on improving self-service options so customers can quickly find answers without needing to contact support. This saves them time and reduces pressure on service teams."
"enhance technical aptitude to resolving customer issues with the product is fast and easy"
"Improving support response times"

❤️ Empathy & Understanding

"Emphasis on listening to the customer's initial problem description and giving them a specific reply, not a template."
"take time to understand who the customer is and what approach he best responds to"
"Preach more empathy for the customers."
